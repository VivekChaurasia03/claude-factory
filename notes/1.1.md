# Chunk 1.1 — Pipeline Overview

## What this is
End-to-end map of how a balloon packet becomes a WeatherMesh forecast input.

## Where it lives in the pipeline
This IS the pipeline — covers every stage from balloon sensor to Atlas API, establishing the mental model for all future work in Stage 3 and Stage 5.

## The production constraint that matters
WeatherMesh pulls every 10 minutes. Every stage — ingestion, QC, ClickHouse write, NetCDF write — must complete inside that window or the forecast goes stale.

## What we built
`pipeline/pipeline_overview.py` — a runnable stage-by-stage breakdown of each component: what comes in, what goes out, why that format, and what breaks if it's wrong. Includes side stores (Redis, Postgres) marked explicitly as not on the critical path.

## What breaks if you get this wrong
Mental model errors here cascade everywhere: treating ClickHouse and S3/NetCDF as interchangeable (they're not — one is for your dashboards, one is what WeatherMesh actually reads), or not knowing where QC lives (consumer, not ingestion) means you'd design replay wrong.

## Remember this
The HTTP ingestion endpoint does nothing except produce to Kafka — all real logic lives in the consumer. QC lives there so you can replay with fixed logic without touching ingestion.
